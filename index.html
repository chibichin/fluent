Why We Delay Important Tasks

People do not always procrastinate because they are lazy. Sometimes they put off a task because it feels overwhelming. A person may worry about making mistakes, receiving criticism, or discovering that the task is more difficult than expected.

Procrastination can provide temporary relief. When people avoid a stressful task, they feel better for a short time. However, the task remains unfinished, and the stress often becomes stronger later.

One useful solution is to break the task into smaller steps. Instead of thinking, “I need to finish the entire report,” a person can begin with, “I will write the first paragraph.” A small action makes it easier to get started.
